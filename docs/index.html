Scandi style

Scandinavian design ― an aesthetic marked by minimalism and functionality ― has been influential since its inception in the early 20th century, but design ideas are still coming out the Nordic region to this day.

Style Trends

Traditionally, only a modest amount of leather could be found in nordic styles, but the Scandinavian trend is now embracing more leather, especially distressed and repurposed styles.

Colors

White walls and cool blue and gray textiles are common. In some homes, brighter pops of color like yellow and orange are found in Marimekko fabrics and rugs.

Materials

Scandinavian design principles promote eco-friendly homes with organic, sustainable building materials for flooring, walls, siding and roofing.
